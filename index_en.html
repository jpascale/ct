TERROIR

Surrounded by an impressive mountain range, Cadus is born in small-scale, groomed vineyards in the most prominent areas of Mendoza. A pronounced and intense luminosity, its low level fertility poor of soil and shortage in water, are notable characteristics that surround this high altitude dessert. In summer time, the ample thermal breadth between night and day, there is a significant thermal amplitude that helps its grapes reach maturity slowly, thus enhancing the varietal purity, color, and aromatic intensity. Both in Lujan de Cuyo or Valle de Uco, each finca vineyard brings invaluable virtues to its core taste, giving gives it it ́s unique and special personality.

FINCA CADUS

Set in the heart of Gualtallary, its sandy soils, which contain containing high level of minerals, enhance its fruity acidity and richness in flavors. In this exclusive land in the department district of Tupungato, well-known wines that gained recognition worldwide recognition first saw the light of day, establishing this region as a trademark for high quality wines. Cadus also finds inspiration in its sister plantation vineyard, Las Torcazas, which is set in Alto Agrelo, Lujan de Cuyo, at 1150 meters in altitude. There, in 2006, a high-density plantation system was built in combination with low production of plants allowing its grapes to reach unbeatable quality.

STORY

Cadus Wines comes to life surrounded by the impressive Andes, in small-scale, grommed farms in Valle de Uco and Luján de Cuyo, in Mendoza. Our grapes mature slowly in this special high altitude dessert where pronounced and intense luminosity, high thermal amplitude between day and night and scarce rains make way for wines of high aromatic intensity and extraordinary color. `Cadus ́ was the Latin name given to amphoras that contained limited batches of a unique wine in ancient Rome. Cadus Wines honours this millenary spirit of creation, seeking, with each wine, the upmost expression of our terroir.
